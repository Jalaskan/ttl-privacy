Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 2026

The short version: TTL does not collect, sell, or share your personal data. Your lessons are yours. We don't read them. Nobody does.

Who we are

Time To Learn ("TTL", "we", "us") is developed and operated by Jamie Anthony Bright, based in the United Kingdom. TTL is an iOS application that generates AI-powered audio lessons on subjects of your choosing, timed to fit your available time.

What data we collect

TTL collects the minimum data necessary to operate the app:

  • Lesson content — the subjects you choose and the lessons generated for you. These are stored in your private iCloud account and are only accessible by you.
  • Usage data — how many minutes of lessons you have generated today. This is stored locally on your device and resets daily.
  • Subscription status — whether you hold an active monthly or annual subscription. This is managed by Apple via StoreKit and we do not store payment information of any kind.
  • Anonymous lesson metadata — when you generate a lesson, an anonymised copy (subject, duration, narrator, tags) is saved to a shared database for the purpose of content curation and app improvement. This contains no personal identifiers.

What data we do NOT collect

  • Your name, email address, or Apple ID
  • Your location or journey details
  • Device identifiers or advertising IDs beyond what Apple's AdMob integration requires
  • Browsing history or behaviour outside of TTL
  • Any biometric or health data

iCloud and Apple services

Your saved lessons and audio files are stored in your personal iCloud account using Apple CloudKit. This data is private — only you can access it. TTL cannot read, access, or retrieve your private iCloud data. Apple's privacy policy governs how iCloud data is handled: apple.com/legal/privacy.

Third-party services

TTL uses the following third-party services to operate:

  • AI content generation — lesson scripts are generated using third-party AI services. Your subject input is sent to these services to produce the lesson text. No personal data beyond your subject query is transmitted.
  • AI voice narration — lesson scripts are converted to audio using third-party AI voice services. The lesson text is sent to produce the audio file. No personal data is transmitted.
  • Unsplash — provides cover photos for lessons based on the subject you choose. A search query is sent to Unsplash. Unsplash's privacy policy applies: unsplash.com/privacy.
  • Google AdMob — displays banner advertisements to free users. AdMob may use device identifiers in accordance with Google's privacy policy: policies.google.com/privacy. Paid subscribers do not see ads.

GDPR — your rights

If you are based in the United Kingdom or European Economic Area, you have the following rights under GDPR:

  • Right to access — you can request a copy of any personal data we hold about you.
  • Right to erasure — you can request deletion of your data at any time. Deleting the app and your iCloud data removes all TTL data associated with you.
  • Right to rectification — you can request correction of any inaccurate data.
  • Right to object — you can object to processing of your data at any time.
  • Right to data portability — you can request your data in a portable format.

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at jamieasbright@gmail.com. We will respond within 30 days.

Data retention

Your private lesson data in iCloud is retained until you delete it. Anonymised lesson metadata in our shared database is retained indefinitely for content curation purposes and contains no personal identifiers. Local usage data resets daily.

Children's privacy

TTL is not directed at children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children. If you believe a child has provided us with personal information, please contact us and we will delete it promptly.

Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. We will notify you of significant changes via the App Store update notes or within the app. Continued use of TTL after changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
